How To Get a Farm Loan

Your guide to agricultural financing

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting Your Farm Loan

Getting a farm loan doesn't have to be complicated. Follow these steps to secure the financing you need for your agricultural property.

Step 1: Determine Your Financing Needs

Before applying, clearly define what you need financing for:

  • Property purchase
  • Refinancing existing debt
  • Property improvements
  • Expansion or development
  • Working capital

Step 2: Gather Your Information

Prepare the following information:

  • Property details and location
  • Property value or purchase price
  • Financial statements (if applicable)
  • Tax returns
  • Business information (for commercial operations)
  • Existing loan information (for refinancing)

Step 3: Choose the Right Loan Type

Select the loan product that best fits your needs:

  • Farm and Ranch Loans for property purchase
  • Farm Equity Line of Credit for flexible access to funds
  • Bridge Loans for short-term needs
  • Specialty loans for groves, vineyards, or horse properties

Step 4: Contact Us

Reach out to us by:

  • Phone: 303-847-0611
  • Email: bob@janusmortgage.com
  • Online: Fill out our pre-qualification form

Step 5: Complete Your Application

Our team will guide you through the application process. We'll help you complete all necessary forms and gather required documentation.

Step 6: Property Evaluation

We'll arrange for a property appraisal with a local appraiser who understands agricultural properties. This ensures accurate valuation.

Step 7: Review and Approval

Our experienced underwriters review your application. We'll keep you informed throughout the process and answer any questions.

Step 8: Closing

Once approved, we'll coordinate the closing process. Our team handles the details to make closing as smooth as possible.

Tips for Success

  • Be prepared with all documentation
  • Be honest about your financial situation
  • Ask questions if you don't understand something
  • Respond promptly to requests for information
  • Work with our team throughout the process
